podcasts I listen to - every episode

June 6, 2020

I stopped commuting when Coranovirus reached us, so I’m not listening to podcasts as much as I used to. I thought now might be a good time to do a list of the ones I like best.

My .opml file, which can be imported into most podcast and rss readers is here:

  • My OPML file of all the podcasts I listen to

I’ve split the list into three posts, depending on whether I listen religiously to every episode, or just most episodes, or whether I cherry pick the ones that interest me:

  • some episodes
  • most episodes
  • every episode

I listen to more-or-less every episode of all of these

Blood on the Tracks - possibly my favorite podcast. Colin Murray’s guests pick their favourite music in categories he dreams up RSS link

Desert Island Discs - I’m slightly obsessed with this RSS link

David Baddiel Tries to Understand - Mr Baddiel learns about some subject, and then presents a 20 minute show about it RSS link

The First Time With… - musicians discuss their musucal firsts (first gig, first record bought etc) with Matt Everitt, I think RSS link

Word Podcast - another muso podcast. A long-time favourite….although I slightly prefer the earlier ones when Mark and Dave were just chewing the fat RSS link

Iain Dale’s Book Club - mainly books about recent political history. I find it fascinating RSS link

At Home With Colin Murray - in-depth interviews with sports people RSS link

The Beatles Podcast - the Fab Four from a Liverpool point-of-view RSS link

Something Rhymes with Purple - Suzie Dent and Gyles Brandreth talk words and language RSS link

Football, Feminism & Everything in Between - Labour bloke Alastair Campbell and comedian Grace Campbell, his daughter RSS link

Unfiltered with James O’Brien - I don’t remember which James O’Brien podcast is which but he’s very good. Another long form interview thing RSS link

Full Disclosure with James O’Brien - See above RSS link

Trolled with Tracy Ann Oberman - Auntie Val from Friday Night Dinner talks about trolling, anti-semitism and stuff. Much more entertaining than that sounds. RSS link

Flintoff, Savage and the Ping Pong Guy - like Kermode and Mayo but sporty. Seems to be defunct, sadly RSS link

Friday Night Comedy from BBC Radio 4 - usually either the News Quiz or the Now Show RSS link

Revisionist History - Malcolm Gladwell ruminates on various subject. I like the description of Gladwell as a public intellectual…partly because it reminds me of Public House, or Public Convenience. The episode on basketball free-throws called something like ‘The Big Man Can’t Shoot’ is a particular favourite RSS link

Dan Snow’s History Hit - Dan Snow comes across as an enthousiastic layman, talking to the experts. It works well RSS link

Inheritance Tracks - a cut-down Desert Island Discs RSS link

Lineker & Baker: Behind Closed Doors - football chat and nonsense RSS link

The Political Party - political interviews by the great Matt Forde RSS link

Political Thinking with Nick Robinson - politcal interviews by the Radio 4 chap RSS link

Great Lives - ex-MP, and current M.P., Matthew Parris talks to someone interesting about someone else interesting RSS link

Fortunately… with Fi and Jane - like Kermode and Mayo but female and more Radio 4. Very entertaining and often informative and educational….to misquote Lord Reith RSS link

Slow Burn - in-depth scandal analysis RSS link

That Peter Crouch Podcast - like Kermode and Mayo but football-centric. RSS link
